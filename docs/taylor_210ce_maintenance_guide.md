# Taylor 210ce Care Guide

## Your Maintenance Kit

- MusicNomad MN241 2 'n 1 Beyond Plush Players Cloth
- MusicNomad MN203 Microfiber Polishing Cloth (3 Pack)
- MusicNomad MN125 F-ONE Unfinished Fretboard Care Kit
- MusicNomad MN100 Premium Guitar Detailer / Cleaner

---

# Cloth Assignment

## Cloth #1 (Daily Use)
Use one MN203 microfiber cloth for:
- Strings after every practice session
- Top
- Back
- Sides
- Neck

## Cloth #2 (Fretboard)
Reserve one MN203 cloth only for fretboard maintenance.

## Cloth #3 (Backup)
Keep as a spare or final buffing cloth.

## MN241 Plush Players Cloth
Keep in your guitar case.
Use for quick wipe-downs immediately after playing.

---

# Daily Routine (30 Seconds)

After every practice:

1. Wipe all strings from bridge to nut.
2. Wipe underneath the strings where possible.
3. Wipe the neck.
4. Wipe the guitar top where your arm rests.
5. Return the guitar to its case.

This alone significantly extends string life.

---

# Weekly Cleaning

1. Spray a small amount of MusicNomad MN100 onto a microfiber cloth.
2. Never spray directly onto the guitar.
3. Wipe:
   - Top
   - Back
   - Sides
   - Neck
4. Use a dry section of the cloth for a final wipe.

Suitable for your Taylor 210ce satin finish.

---

# Fretboard Maintenance

Frequency:
- Every 6-12 months
- Or when the ebony looks dry, gray, or chalky

## Procedure

1. Remove all strings.
2. Use the brush from the MN125 kit to loosen dirt.
3. Apply a very small amount of F-ONE oil to the dedicated fretboard cloth.
4. Rub each fret space following the wood grain.
5. Allow it to sit for 1-2 minutes.
6. Wipe off ALL excess oil.
7. Let the fretboard rest for several minutes.
8. Install new strings.

Important:
- Less is more.
- Do not soak the fretboard.
- Do not apply F-ONE to the body, neck finish, or headstock.

---

# String Change Day Routine

1. Remove old strings.
2. Clean guitar with MN100.
3. Clean fretboard with brush.
4. Apply F-ONE if needed.
5. Install new strings.
6. Tune to pitch.
7. Stretch strings gently.
8. Retune.
