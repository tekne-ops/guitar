# Guitar

Personal practice notes and resources for worship guitar fingerpicking — building confidence to lead praise in church with fingerstyle arpeggios and a focused song repertoire.

## Overview

This repository tracks a structured comeback plan for fingerpicking worship guitar. The goal is not just technical accuracy, but expressive, intentional playing suited to intimate and prayerful worship settings.

**Full plan:** [docs/worship_guitar_plan.md](docs/worship_guitar_plan.md)

## Practice routine

**Time:** 25–30 minutes, 5 days per week

| Block | Duration | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **A. Warm-up** | 5 min | C → G → Am → F with basic pattern; clean tone, even timing |
| **B. Core skills** | 10 min | Two fingerpicking patterns per week across rotating chord progressions |
| **C. Worship application** | 10–15 min | One hymn + one contemporary song, using at least two patterns |
| **D. Creative time** *(optional)* | 3–5 min | Free improvisation over chord loops |

### Weekly chord rotations

- **Week 1:** G – D – Em – C
- **Week 2:** C – G – Am – F
- **Week 3:** D – A – Bm – G

## Fingerpicking patterns

Notation uses classical finger labels: **P** (thumb), **i** (index), **m** (middle), **a** (ring).

| # | Pattern | Best for |
|---|---------|----------|
| 1 | `P – i – m – a – m – i` | Slow worship, prayer |
| 2 | `P – i – P – m – P – a` | Hymns, fuller sound |
| 3 | `P – i – m – i – m – a` | Contemporary songs |
| 4 | `P – m – i – m` | Soft, emotional sections |
| 5 | `P – i – m \| P – i – m` | 3/4 waltz feel (e.g. Amazing Grace) |

## Growth milestones

- **Weeks 1–2:** Control and accuracy
- **Weeks 3–4:** Dynamics and expression
- **Week 5+:** Integrate singing while playing

**Avoid:** mechanical repetition without feeling.  
**Focus:** connecting technique to the spiritual moment.
